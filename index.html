About Me

Hi, I'm Bern De Nijs, a game development student at Digital Arts and Entertainment (DAE) in Belgium. I'm passionate about creating engaging and innovative games, and I specialize in both design and programming. I am constantly working on improving my skills and exploring new game mechanics, tools, and creative ideas.

Projects

Patchwork Parkour

Patchwork Parkour is a couch co-op game where you and your friends each control a separate limb of a character called "Franky". Working together, you must navigate and climb to the top of a challenging tower filled with obstacles. I worked as the lead designer and programmer, focusing on the climbing mechanics, obstacle design, and multiplayer functionality.

You can check out the game on itch.io.

Doppler

Doppler is a physics-based combat game where you wield a gun that can push or pull objects. Use this mechanic to create mayhem in the environment and engage in creative combat. This project was done solo as part of a course assignment, allowing me to explore unique physics-driven mechanics.

WaitASec

WaitASec is a time manipulation game where you can freeze time to plan a series of quick, time-based abilities to defeat your enemies. The ability to stop time allows for strategic planning and fast execution. I developed this game solo for a course project, experimenting with time-based gameplay mechanics.

PacmanGameEngine

PacmanGameEngine is a barebones game engine I created with a team to replicate a basic Pac-Man style game. This project was a valuable learning experience in understanding the fundamentals of game engines, game loops, and rendering processes.
