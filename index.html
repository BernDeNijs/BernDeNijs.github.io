About Me

Hi, I'm Bern De Nijs, a game development student at Digital Arts and Entertainment (DAE) in Belgium.
My whole life I've been engrossed in the world of video games and I've spent the last few years eagerly learning how to make them myself.
I have a few small projects under my belt now.
Mostly small games made in a few days for some of our courses but also a few larger projects I've worked on with a team.
Most of my time these days is spent working for school projects but in my spare time I still work on a personal project with a group of friends.
I hope to release it in a year or two.

Projects

Patchwork Parkour

Info

Patchwork Parkour is a couch co-op game where you and your friends each control a separate limb of a character called "Franky". Working together, you must navigate and climb to the top of a challenging tower filled with obstacles.

I worked as the lead designer and programmer, focusing on the climbing mechanics, obstacle design, and multiplayer functionality.

Play on itch.io

Doppler

Info

Doppler is a physics-based combat game where you wield a gun that can push or pull objects. Use this mechanic to create mayhem in the environment and engage in creative combat.

This project was done solo as part of a course assignment, allowing me to explore unique physics-driven mechanics.

Play on itch.io

Other Projects

WaitASec

WaitASec is a time manipulation game where you can freeze time to plan a series of quick, time-based abilities to defeat your enemies. The ability to stop time allows for strategic planning and fast execution.

I developed this game solo for a course project, experimenting with time-based gameplay mechanics.

Play on itch.io

PacmanGameEngine

PacmanGameEngine is a first attempt at writing my own game engine which I used to make a short level of pacman. This project was a valuable learning experience in understanding the fundamentals of game engines and game loops.
